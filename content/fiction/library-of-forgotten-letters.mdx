---
title: "The Library of Forgotten Letters"
excerpt: "A short story about a woman who curates correspondence that was never sent."
date: "Aug 12, 2024"
---

The archive was located in the basement of an old postal building. It smelled of dry paper, ink, and regret. 

Elara spent her days sorting through boxes of letters that had been undeliverable, unclaimed, or explicitly marked "do not send" before being abandoned in dead-letter offices. Her job was to catalog them.

There were angry letters written in the heat of a moment, full of vitriol and sharp edges, that the writer clearly thought better of sending. There were confessions of love that were deemed too risky, too vulnerable to trust to the mail. There were apologies written years too late, addressed to people who had likely moved on or passed away.

One Tuesday, Elara found a letter addressed to herself.

It was in a familiar handwriting—her father's. The postmark was dated ten years ago, the week before he died. The "Return to Sender" stamp was stark red against the faded envelope. She held it in her hands, the weight of a decade's silence pressing down on her. 

She realized then that the archive was not a graveyard of words. It was a holding pen for ghosts, waiting for the right moment to finally speak. She took a breath, reached for her letter opener, and broke the seal.
